Selena Marie Gomez (/səˈliːnə ˈɡoʊmɛz/ sə-LEE-nə GOH-mez; born July 22, 1992) is an American singer, actress, businesswoman and producer. Throughout her career, Gomez has won numerous accolades including: an American Music Award, two MTV Video Music Awards, broke 16 Guinness World Records, and was nominated for two Grammy Awards, a Golden Globe Award, and four Emmy Awards. Gomez is one of the most-influential person in the social media; currently she is the most-followed woman on Instagram with over 420 million followers, and one of the most-followed person on Twitter. Gomez was included in Time's annual list of the 100 most influential people in the world (2020), and was named Woman of the Year (2017). Gomez began her career as a child actress, starring on the children's television series Barney & Friends (2002–2004). Gomez formed the pop rock band Selena Gomez & the Scene through her record deal with Hollywood Records.[7][8] After releasing three studio albums, she confirmed in early 2012 that she would be taking a break from music, placing the group on hiatus.[9] Despite earlier claims, she began to work on solo material.[10] Her first solo studio album, Stars Dance, was released in July 2013, and debuted atop the US Billboard 200.[11] Its lead single, "Come & Get It", reached the top ten in the US, Canada, and the United Kingdom.[12] Having spent seven years with Hollywood Records, the singer signed a recording contract with Interscope Records in 2014.[13] To officially end her contract with her former label, she released the compilation album For You (2014), which spawned the US top-ten single "The Heart Wants What It Wants".[6]
